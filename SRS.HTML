Abstract

PestiVid is an innovative Web3 demonstration platform designed to bring transparency and trust to the agricultural supply chain. It leverages blockchain-simulated transactions, decentralized video storage (IPFS) and a suite of sophisticated AI tools to create a secure ecosystem for farmers, buyers and investors. Farmers record immutable video evidence of their crops and practices (Proof of-Practice). Buyers gain access to verified information, enabling confident purchasing. Investors discover and fund agricultural projects with enhanced operational visibility. The platform integrates flexible user authentication and secure messaging. Its core innovation lies in its AI capabilities which include a multi-stage plant disease identification system utilizing CLIP and Deepseek models for accurate diagnosis and treatment recommendation and an advanced agricultural chatbot (AgriBot) powered by a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) architecture using a Deepseek model to provide context-aware, reliable advice. PestiVid addresses information asymmetry and market access challenges, fostering a more equitable and sustainable agricultural marketplace.

Chapter 1: Introduction

1.1 Problem Statement

The traditional agricultural supply chain often suffers from a critical lack of transparency, making it difficult for buyers to verify claims about product origin, quality, and farming practices. This information asymmetry leads to reduced consumer trust and unfair pricing for farmers who employ sustainable or premium methods. Investors also face significant challenges in identifying and tracking legitimate agricultural projects, which increases their financial risk.

Furthermore, smallholder farmers frequently lack direct access to broader markets, efficient funding mechanisms and timely expert agricultural advice. PestiVid aims to solve these problems by creating a Web3 enabled platform that enhances transparency through video-verified proof, builds trust using decentralized data principles, provides farmers with direct market access and empowers them with state of the art AI driven support tools.

1.2 Project Scope & Objectives

The scope of this PestiVid demo is to build a functional proof-of-concept that showcases the potential of a transparent, Web3-based agricultural ecosystem. The key objectives are:

  • Develop a multi-role platform for Farmers, Buyers, and Investors with distinct, role-based functionalities.
  • Implement "Proof-of-Practice" by allowing farmers to record video, calculate a file hash (SHA256), and upload it to decentralized storage (.
  • Create a transparent marketplace (AgriSell) where produce is listed with verifiable video evidence, and buyers can perform simulated purchases.
  • Build an investment module where farmers can request project funding and investors can browse opportunities and simulate investments.
  • Integrate advanced AI tools for practical farmer support, including:
    • A multi-model AI pipeline for plant disease analysis, using a CLIP model for initial identification and a Deepseek model for detailed treatment recommendations.
    • An advanced AgriBot chatbot using a **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)framework with a Deepseek model to ensure responses are accurate and grounded in a specific knowledge base.
  • Demonstrate Web3 principles through dual authentication and simulated blockchain transactions for key events.

1.3 User Classes and Characteristics

  1. Farmers: The primary producers. They seek to showcase their sustainable practices, achieve fairer prices, and access direct funding. They require intuitive tools for video creation, listing management, and accessing AI-driven farm support.
  2. Buyers: Individuals or businesses seeking high-quality produce. They value transparency, verification of claims e.g., "organic", and direct sourcing. They need an easy way to browse, view video proof, and complete a simple (simulated) purchase.
  3. Investors: Individuals or entities looking to fund agricultural projects. They seek transparent, verifiable project details and clear ROI information to de-risk their investments. They need a platform to discover projects and track their (simulated) financial performance.

Chapter 2: Overall Description & Planning

2.1 Product Perspective & System Overview

PestiVid is designed as a self-contained, client-side demonstration to illustrate the core value proposition of a trust-based agricultural platform. It is a proof-of-concept that integrates multiple modern web technologies to simulate a real-world ecosystem. While not intended for production use in its current form, its modular features could be extracted and adapted for larger, enterprise-grade systems.

2.2 Project Phases and Modules

The project was developed in logical phases:
  • Phase 1: Foundation & User Management: Establishing the Vue.js project, implementing Orange ID/Solana authentication, and creating the core user profile and navigation structure.
  • Phase 2: Core Farmer Features: Building the PestiVid video recording/upload module, the AgriStream gallery, and the forms for creating marketplace listings and funding requests.
  • Phase 3: Buyer & Investor Features: Developing the AgriSell marketplace for buyers and the project discovery/investment portal for investors, including portfolio management.
  • Phase 4: AI & Communication Integration: Integrating the CLIP and Deepseek models for the PlantRecommend and AgriBot features, and building the messaging and notification systems.
  • Phase 5: Testing & Refinement: End-to-end testing of all user flows, bug fixing, and UI/UX improvements.

2.3 Design and Implementation Constraints

  1. Technology Stack: The project is constrained to the chosen stack: Vue.js 2, Tailwind CSS, and specific external APIs (Orange ID, Solana, Pinata, Deepseek/CLIP APIs).
  2. Client-Side Architecture: As a demo, all logic and data persistence localStorageare handled on the client side. This is not scalable or secure for a production environment.
  3. Simulated Blockchain: All blockchain interactions are simulated. There are no real-world cryptographic transactions or asset transfers.
  4. API Key Management: A critical constraint. All API keys are stored client-side for ease of demonstration. In a production system, this is a major security vulnerability, and keys **MUST** be managed on a secure backend server.
  5. Public Data on IPFS: Users must be aware that any video uploaded to IPFS is publicly accessible via its CID.

Chapter 3: System Requirements & Features

This chapter details the functional requirements of the PestiVid platform, broken down by core feature.

3.1 User Authentication and Role Management

Description: Provides secure user access via multiple Web3 methods and establishes user roles within the platform.
Priority: High.

Functional Requirements:

  • REQ-3.1.1: The system must support user authentication via Orange ID.
  • REQ-3.1.2: The system must support user authentication by connecting a Solana browser wallet.
  • REQ-3.1.3: First-time users, after authentication, must be prompted to select a primary role (Farmer, Buyer, or Investor).

3.2 Farmer: Video Recording & IPFS Upload (PestiVid Module)

Description: Enables farmers to create the core "Proof-of-Practice" asset.
Priority: High.

Functional Requirements:

  • REQ-3.2.1: The system shall allow authenticated Farmers to record video using their device's camera via WebRTC.
  • REQ-3.2.2: Before upload, the system must calculate the SHA256 hash of the video file for integrity verification.
  • REQ-3.2.3: The system must upload the video file and associated metadata to IPFS using the Pinata API.

3.3 Farmer: Marketplace Listing (AgriSell Module)

Description: Allows farmers to monetize their produce by creating listings on the AgriSell marketplace.
Priority: High.

Functional Requirements:

  • REQ-3.3.1: The system must allow a Farmer to select one of their unlisted PestiVid videos to create a new listing.
  • REQ-3.3.2: The Farmer must be able to set a minimum and maximum selling price in simulated SOL.

3.4 Farmer: AI-Powered Support Tools

Description: Provides farmers with advanced, multi-stage AI tools for decision-making.
Priority: High.

Functional Requirements:

  • REQ-3.4.1 (PlantRecommend): The system shall allow a Farmer to upload an image of a plant.
  • REQ-3.4.2 (PlantRecommend): The system must first process the image with a CLIP model to identify the potential disease. The resulting disease name is then passed to a Deepseek model via an AI agent to generate a detailed treatment recommendation.
  • REQ-3.4.3 (AgriBot): The system must provide a chat interface for farmers to ask agricultural questions.
  • REQ-3.4.4 (AgriBot): The system must process user queries through a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system, which retrieves relevant information from a knowledge base and uses a Deepseek model to generate a final, contextually aware answer.

3.5 Buyer: Marketplace and Purchase

Description: Enables buyers to find and confidently purchase produce.
Priority: High.

Functional Requirements:

  • REQ-3.5.1: The system must display all active marketplace listings to authenticated users.
  • REQ-3.5.2: Buyers must be able to view the full details of a listing, including playing the associated IPFS video.
  • REQ-3.5.3: Authenticated Buyers must be able to initiate a simulated purchase.

3.6 Investor: Project Discovery and Investment

Description: Enables investors to fund agricultural projects.
Priority: High.

Functional Requirements:

  • REQ-3.6.1: The system must display all active funding requests to authenticated users.
  • REQ-3.6.2: Authenticated Investors must be able to perform a simulated investment in a project and track it in a personal portfolio.

3.7 Cross-Role: Messaging & Notifications

Description: Facilitates communication and provides user feedback.
Priority: Medium.

Functional Requirements:

  • REQ-3.7.1: The system must support private, one-to-one text messaging between authenticated users.
  • REQ-3.7.2: The system must display non-intrusive toast notifications to provide feedback on user actions.

Chapter 4: External Interface Requirements

4.1 User Interfaces

The system shall provide a graphical user interface (GUI) built as a responsive web application. The UI must be intuitive and adapt to various screen sizes (desktop and mobile). Key screens include: Login Page, Role-specific Dashboards, PestiVid Recording/Upload Page, Marketplace (AgriSell), Project Funding Page, Portfolio and Messaging Interface.

4.2 Software & Hardware Interfaces

  • Software Interfaces: The application interfaces with modern web browsers (e.g., Chrome, Firefox) that support JavaScript, WebRTC and localStorage. It communicates with several external software APIs via HTTPS:
    • Orange ID (Bedrock SDK): For identity verification.
    • Solana Wallet Browser Extension: For wallet connection and signing (simulated).
    • Pinata API: For uploading files to IPFS.
    • CLIP & Deepseek Model APIs: For AI-powered plant analysis and chatbot functionality.
  • Hardware Interfaces: The system requires access to the user's device camera and microphone (for video recording). No other special hardware is needed.

Chapter 5: Nonfunctional Requirements

5.1 Performance Requirements

The user interface should be responsive, with UI interactions completing in under 200ms. Page load times should be under 5 seconds on a standard internet connection. API response times for AI services are dependent on the external provider but should include clear loading indicators in the UI. Video upload performance depends on the user's network speed and file size, and the system must provide continuous feedback during the process.

5.2 Security Requirements

User authentication will be managed by trusted third-party services. All communication with external APIs must be conducted over HTTPS. As this is a client-side demo, API keys are stored in the frontend code for a production release, all keys MUST be moved to a secure backend server to prevent exposure. Input from users should be sanitized to prevent cross-site scripting, although Vue.js provides some default protection.

5.3 Software Quality Attributes

  • Usability: The platform must be intuitive for users with varying levels of technical expertise.
  • Reliability: The application should handle API errors gracefully, providing clear error messages without crashing.
  • Maintainability: The code is structured into logical Vue components to facilitate future modifications.

Chapter 6: Implementation & Technology Stack

PestiVid is developed as a single-page web application using Vue.js for a reactive frontend. Styling is handled by Tailwind CSS. The core implementation relies on JavaScript to orchestrate interactions between the user, the browser, and external APIs.

Key technologies integrated are:

  • Frontend Framework: Vue.js (v2.6.14)
  • Styling: Tailwind CSS (v2.2.19)
  • Authentication: Orange ID (via Bedrock Passport SDK) and Solana Wallet (via @solana/web3.js).
  • Decentralized Storage: IPFS via the Pinata API.
  • AI Integration:
    • Plant Disease Identification: A two-stage pipeline. A CLIP model performs initial image-to-text classification to identify the disease. An AI agent then uses this output to query a Deepseek model for specific, actionable treatment advice.
    • AgriBot Chatbot: Utilizes a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) architecture. User queries retrieve relevant documents from a knowledge base (conceptual), which are then fed as context to a **Deepseek large language model** to generate accurate, source-grounded answers.
  • Data Persistence: Browser localStorage.

Chapter 7: Testing Strategy

A comprehensive testing strategy is crucial for a multi-faceted application like PestiVid. The conceptual testing plan includes:

  • Unit Testing: Testing individual functions and components in isolation (e.g., verifying the calculateFileHash function, testing form validation logic).
  • Integration Testing: Verifying the interactions between different modules, such as ensuring a video uploaded via the PestiVid module correctly appears as an option in the Sell module. This also includes testing the integration with the CLIP and Deepseek APIs to ensure requests and responses are handled correctly.
  • End-to-End (E2E) Testing: Simulating full user journeys, such as a farmer registering, uploading a video, listing it for sale, and a buyer logging in and simulating a purchase.
  • Usability Testing: Gathering feedback from representative users on the clarity and ease-of-use of the platform for each of the three roles.

Chapter 8: Conclusion

8.1 Project Achievements

The PestiVid project successfully demonstrates a powerful proof-of-concept for a transparent, Web3-enabled agricultural platform. It achieves its primary goal of integrating decentralized identity, immutable video storage, and advanced AI into a cohesive application. A key achievement is the implementation of a sophisticated, multi-model AI pipeline, using CLIP and Deepseek models for plant analysis and a RAG-based Deepseek model for the AgriBot. This showcases a significant step beyond generic AI integration, proving the viability of the "Proof-of-Practice" concept as a foundation for building trust.

8.2 Future Enhancements

  1. Full Blockchain Integration: Migrate simulated transactions to real smart contracts on a high-performance blockchain like Solana for true decentralization.
  2. Scalable Backend Infrastructure: Replace localStorage with a robust backend and database solution. Move all API key management to this secure server environment.
  3. Expand RAG Knowledge Base: Continuously update the knowledge base for the AgriBot with regional agricultural data, new research, and farmer-contributed information to improve its accuracy and relevance.
  4. Tokenization & DeFi Integration: Introduce a platform utility token for governance and staking. Integrate with DeFi protocols for escrow services.
  5. Mobile Application: Develop native mobile apps to improve the user experience in the field.
