Abstract

PestiVid is an innovative Web3 demonstration platform designed to bring transparency and trust to the agricultural supply chain. It leverages blockchain-simulated transactions, decentralized video storage (IPFS), and AI-powered support tools to create a secure and efficient ecosystem for farmers, buyers, and investors. Farmers can record video evidence of their crops and farming practices, storing this data immutably. Buyers gain access to verified information, enabling confident purchasing decisions. Investors can discover and fund agricultural projects with enhanced visibility into operations and potential returns. The platform integrates user authentication via Orange ID and Solana wallets, secure messaging, real-time notifications, and AI-driven features like plant disease recommendation (using Google Gemini) and an agricultural chatbot (AgriBot, powered by Groq). PestiVid aims to address common challenges in agriculture such as information asymmetry, lack of traceability, and difficult market access, ultimately fostering a more equitable and sustainable agricultural marketplace.

Chapter 1: Introduction & Communication

1.1 Client Definition (User Roles)

PestiVid serves three primary user roles, each with distinct needs and interactions within the platform:

1. Farmers:

  • Description: Agricultural producers seeking to showcase their products, practices, and gain fair market access or funding.
  • Needs:
    • A simple way to record and upload video evidence of crops and practices (PestiVid & AgriStream).
    • A platform to list their produce for sale directly to buyers (Sell).
    • Opportunities to request and receive funding for their projects (Get Funding).
    • Access to tools for farm management support, like plant disease identification (PlantRecommend) and expert advice (AgriBot).
    • Secure communication with buyers and investors.

2. Buyers:

  • Description: Individuals or businesses looking to purchase agricultural products with greater transparency and assurance of quality/origin.
  • Needs:
    • Access to a marketplace with video-verified listings (AgriSell).
    • Detailed information about crop origin, farming practices, and pesticide use.
    • Confidence in the authenticity and traceability of purchased goods.
    • Direct communication with farmers.

3. Investors:

  • Description: Individuals or entities interested in funding agricultural projects with transparent tracking and potential returns.
  • Needs:
    • A platform to discover and evaluate agricultural projects seeking investment (Projects).
    • Video evidence and detailed information about funded projects.
    • Transparent tracking of investment progress and (simulated) returns (Portfolio).
    • Secure communication with farmers.

1.3 Problem Statement

The traditional agricultural supply chain often suffers from a lack of transparency, making it difficult for buyers to verify claims about product origin, quality, and farming practices. This information asymmetry can lead to reduced trust and unfair pricing for farmers who employ sustainable or premium methods. Investors also face challenges in identifying and tracking legitimate agricultural projects, increasing their risk.

Furthermore, farmers, especially smallholders, may lack easy access to direct markets, efficient funding mechanisms, and timely, expert agricultural advice. Existing platforms might not fully leverage modern technologies like decentralized storage and AI to address these issues comprehensively.

PestiVid aims to solve these problems by creating a Web3-enabled platform that:

  • Enhances transparency through video-verified proof of crop and practices.
  • Builds trust between farmers, buyers, and investors using (simulated) blockchain principles and decentralized data.
  • Provides farmers with direct market access and alternative funding avenues.
  • Empowers farmers with AI-driven support tools for better decision-making.

1.4 Background & Motivation

The global agricultural sector is undergoing significant transformation, driven by increasing consumer demand for food safety, traceability, and sustainability. There's a growing interest in understanding where food comes from and how it's produced. Simultaneously, technologies like blockchain and IPFS offer new paradigms for creating secure, transparent, and decentralized systems. Web3 principles encourage user ownership and control over data and interactions.

The motivation behind PestiVid stems from the desire to harness these technological advancements to empower participants in the agricultural ecosystem. By providing tools for verifiable proof, direct communication, and transparent transactions, PestiVid seeks to create a fairer and more efficient marketplace. The integration of AI tools further aims to provide valuable, accessible support to farmers, enhancing their productivity and sustainability.

1.5 Project Objectives

The primary objectives of the PestiVid project are to:
  • Develop a secure and user-friendly platform for farmers to record, upload (to IPFS), and manage video evidence of their agricultural activities.
  • Implement a marketplace (AgriSell) where farmers can list their video-verified crops for sale, and buyers can browse and purchase with confidence.
  • Create a module for farmers to request funding for their projects, supported by video evidence, and for investors to discover and fund these opportunities.
  • Integrate dual authentication methods (Orange ID and Solana Wallet) for flexible user access.
  • Enable secure peer-to-peer messaging between platform users.
  • Implement AI-powered features:
    • Plant disease detection and treatment recommendation using image analysis (Google Gemini).
    • An agricultural assistant chatbot (AgriBot) for farming advice (Groq).
  • Simulate blockchain interactions for key transactions (listings, purchases, investments) to demonstrate Web3 concepts.
  • Provide a clear user interface with role-specific dashboards and real-time (simulated) notifications.
  • Ensure data persistence for the demo using browser local storage.

This activity diagram would illustrate the steps a farmer takes from recording a video to listing their crop on the marketplace.

  1. Farmer navigates to "PestiVid" section.
  2. Farmer inputs crop details (type, location, pesticide, etc.).
  3. Farmer initiates video recording.
  4. System accesses camera.
  5. Farmer records video.
  6. Farmer stops recording.
  7. Video is previewed and (locally) saved.
  8. Farmer initiates upload to IPFS.
  9. System uploads video and metadata (including SHA256 hash) to IPFS via Pinata.
  10. IPFS returns CID.
  11. Video (with CID & hash) is added to Farmer's AgriStream.
  12. Farmer navigates to "Sell" section.
  13. Farmer selects the uploaded video (by CID) for listing.
  14. Farmer inputs pricing details (min/max price).
  15. Farmer submits listing.
  16. System creates listing, (simulates) blockchain transaction, and makes it visible on AgriSell.
  17. (Optional) System notifies potential buyers.

Chapter 4: Implementation & Coding

PestiVid is developed as a single-page web application using Vue.js (version 2.6.14) for the frontend. Styling is primarily handled by Tailwind CSS (version 2.2.19) for rapid and responsive UI development, with Font Awesome (version 6.5.2) for icons.

Key technologies and libraries integrated include:

  • Authentication:
    • Orange ID: Leveraged via the Bedrock Passport SDK for social logins and email-based authentication.
    • Solana Wallet: Direct integration with browser-based Solana wallets (e.g., Phantom) using `@solana/web3.js` for Web3 native login.
  • Decentralized Storage:
    • IPFS: Videos are uploaded to the InterPlanetary File System via the Pinata pinning service. Axios is used for HTTP requests to the Pinata API. The SHA256 hash of the video file is calculated client-side before upload for integrity verification.
  • Blockchain Interaction (Simulated):
    • While full blockchain deployment is outside the demo scope, key transactions (listing creation, purchases, investments) are simulated to mimic on-chain events, generating mock transaction hashes.
  • AI Integrations:
    • Google Gemini API: Used for the Plant Recommendation feature. Plant images uploaded by users are sent to the Gemini model for analysis (plant identification, disease detection, treatment suggestions).
    • Groq API: Powers the AgriBot chatbot, providing farmers with AI-driven agricultural advice using a Llama3-based model.
  • Data Persistence (Demo):
    • Browser Local Storage: Used to store application data (user profiles, video metadata, listings, funding requests, messages, etc.) for persistence across sessions in this demo version.
  • Other Libraries: `marked.js` for rendering Markdown in the AgriBot chat.

Chapter 5: Testing Strategy

5.1 Importance of Testing in Web3 dApps

Testing is paramount in all software development, but it takes on heightened importance in Web3 decentralized applications (dApps) and platforms interacting with blockchain and AI services. For PestiVid, thorough testing ensures:

  • Security: Validating authentication flows (Orange ID, Solana), ensuring role-based access control, and protecting user data are critical. For services interacting with real assets (even if simulated here), security against unauthorized actions is vital.
  • Reliability: Ensuring consistent video uploads to IPFS, correct metadata association, reliable API interactions with Gemini and Groq, and robust (simulated) transaction processing.
  • Data Integrity: Verifying that video file hashes match, and that data displayed to users (listings, investment progress) is accurate and reflects the underlying (simulated) state.
  • User Experience (UX): Given the complexity of Web3 interactions and multiple user roles, ensuring an intuitive and smooth UX across all features (video recording, marketplace, funding, AI tools) is crucial for adoption.
  • Smart Contract Logic (Future): Although PestiVid uses simulated blockchain, if it were to move to real smart contracts, testing their logic would be extremely critical due to the immutability of deployed contracts.

5.2 Testing Report for PestiVid (Conceptual)

A comprehensive testing strategy for PestiVid would include the following types of tests:

1. Unit Testing

  • Objective: Test individual Vue components, utility functions, and API service wrappers in isolation.
  • Examples:
    • Verify the `calculateFileHash` function produces correct SHA256 hashes.
    • Test input validation in forms (e.g., price ranges, funding amounts).
    • Mock API calls to Pinata, Gemini, Groq to test response handling in service methods.
    • Test Vue component rendering based on different props and data states.

2. Integration Testing

  • Objective: Test interactions between different modules and services.
  • Examples:
    • User authentication flow: Orange ID/Solana login leading to correct role-based dashboard access.
    • PestiVid to AgriSell flow: Farmer uploads video, video appears in AgriStream, farmer successfully lists it, and it appears in AgriSell for buyers.
    • Gemini API integration: Farmer uploads plant image, request is sent to Gemini, response is parsed and displayed correctly.
    • Groq API integration: User sends message to AgriBot, request to Groq, response displayed in chat.
    • Simulated purchase flow: Buyer initiates purchase, (simulated) payment, listing removal, and notifications.

3. End-to-End (E2E) Testing

  • Objective: Test complete user flows from the user's perspective, simulating real interactions.
  • Examples:
    • A farmer registers, records a video, uploads it, lists it for sale, and another user (buyer) logs in, finds the listing, and (simulates) a purchase.
    • An investor logs in, browses funding projects, invests in one, and sees their portfolio updated.

4. Usability Testing

  • Objective: Evaluate the ease of use and intuitiveness of the platform for each user role.
  • Methods: Observing users (or peers) performing common tasks, collecting feedback on UI clarity, navigation, and overall experience.

5. Security Testing (Conceptual for Demo)

  • Objective: Identify potential vulnerabilities.
  • Areas of Focus:
    • Authentication & Authorization: Ensure only authenticated users with correct roles can access specific features/data.
    • Input Validation: Check for potential issues if malicious input is provided (though client-side only in this demo).
    • API Key Exposure: (As noted, a critical issue in the current codebase if it were production).

6. API Interaction Testing

  • Objective: Verify correct request formation and response handling for all external APIs (Pinata, Gemini, Groq).
  • Methods: Mocking API responses for various scenarios (success, error, rate limiting) to ensure the application handles them gracefully.

Chapter 6: Conclusion

6.1 Summary

PestiVid, as a Web3 demonstration platform, successfully showcases the potential of integrating blockchain principles (simulated), decentralized storage (IPFS), and artificial intelligence to enhance transparency, trust, and efficiency in the agricultural sector. The project provides distinct functionalities for Farmers, Buyers, and Investors, centered around video-verified evidence of farming practices. Key features include robust user authentication (Orange ID & Solana), video recording and IPFS upload, a marketplace for crop sales, a platform for agricultural project funding, AI-driven plant diagnosis, and an AI chatbot for farming assistance.

Through its various modules, PestiVid demonstrates a user-centric approach, addressing specific pain points for each user role. The use of Vue.js and Tailwind CSS allowed for rapid development of a responsive and interactive user interface. The integration of external APIs like Pinata, Google Gemini, and Groq highlights the power of composability in modern web development to deliver sophisticated features.

6.2 Project Achievements for PestiVid

  1. Multi-Role Platform: Successfully implemented distinct workflows and interfaces for Farmers, Buyers, and Investors.
  2. Dual Authentication System: Integrated both Orange ID (social/email) and Solana wallet-based authentication, offering flexibility.
  3. Video Verification & IPFS Storage: Enabled farmers to record videos and upload them to IPFS via Pinata, with client-side SHA256 hash calculation for (conceptual) integrity.
  4. Functional Marketplace (Simulated): Created AgriSell where farmers can list produce and buyers can (simulate) purchases.
  5. Investment Module (Simulated): Developed a system for farmers to request funding and investors to fund projects, with portfolio tracking.
  6. AI-Powered Plant Recommendation: Integrated Google Gemini API for plant image analysis, providing disease detection and treatment suggestions.
  7. AI Farming Assistant (AgriBot): Integrated Groq API to power a chatbot offering agricultural advice.
  8. User Communication: Implemented a peer-to-peer messaging system and (simulated) real-time notifications.
  9. Responsive UI: Developed a user-friendly interface using Vue.js and Tailwind CSS.
  10. Web3 Concepts Demonstration: Effectively simulated blockchain transactions and showcased the utility of decentralized storage for a practical use case.

6.3 Future Enhancements for PestiVid

  1. Real Blockchain Integration: Transition from simulated transactions to actual smart contracts on a blockchain (e.g., Solana, Polygon, or an L2 solution) for listings, purchases, and investment management.
  2. Tokenization: Introduce a utility token for platform governance, staking, or premium features. Potentially tokenize agricultural assets or investment shares.
  3. Enhanced AI Capabilities:
    • More sophisticated plant analysis (e.g., nutrient deficiency, pest identification).
    • Personalized advice from AgriBot based on farmer's specific location, crops, and history.
    • AI-driven market price predictions or demand forecasting.
  4. Supply Chain Tracking: Extend blockchain use to track produce from farm to consumer for full traceability.
  5. Mobile Application: Develop native mobile apps for easier field use, especially for video recording and AI tool access.
  6. Community Features: Forums, farmer groups, expert Q&A sessions to foster a collaborative ecosystem.
  7. Data Analytics Dashboard: Provide farmers and investors with more detailed analytics on their activities, market trends, and project performance.
  8. Third-Party Integrations: Connect with weather APIs, soil sensor data, or existing farm management software.
  9. Robust Security Audit: If moving to real assets, a comprehensive security audit of smart contracts and platform infrastructure would be essential.
  10. Scalable Backend: Move data persistence from local storage to a scalable database solution if user base grows. Secure API key management via a dedicated backend.

6.4 Final Thoughts & Learnings

The PestiVid project served as an excellent exploration into building a multifaceted Web3-inspired application. It highlighted the complexities of integrating various cutting-edge technologiesâ€”decentralized identity (Orange ID), wallet-based authentication (Solana), decentralized storage (IPFS), and multiple AI APIs (Gemini, Groq)â€”into a cohesive user experience.
